Are Battle Royal Games still Fun?

By: Oluwanifemi O. Olotu

Published: June 1st 2022

If you have managed to click onto this website, then you most likely know what a battle royal is, or at the
very least have heard of it. For those who do not know, and for the sake of proper context in this article,
a battle royal is a form of game which involves a relatively large number of players competing to be
the last player to survive. There may be other definitions out there, but let us stick with this for now. To make
it easier for you to remember, think of The Hunger Games when you see battle royal as it is essentially
the same concept and if you have not seen The Hunger Games, then I am not sure what to tell you.

The concept of hosting a battle royal in the real world is not necessarily the most practical or ethical
thing to do, given that elimination requires one loosing their life, but the concept remains quite
entertaining. To give people the rush of fighting for survival without having to risk ones life, video game
companies have created virtual versions of battle royals, where various players fight it out to be either
the last player or team standing. A great Idea of course, but because the methods, means and skills used
to survive in such a game become more and more complex over time, players who are accustomed to
the system become better, further increasing their competitive advantage. This is most definitely a good
thing for such a player, but this also makes it more difficult for new players to find fun in the game as he
or she is surrounded by players that are significantly better. I know what you are thinking, “Is that not
what skill-based match making is for?”, well yes, but people get super lucky for a couple of games
which moves them up the skill ladder, however they are unable to move back down when their luck runs out.
This leaves players stuck in a position that causes them to slowly drift away from the game.
